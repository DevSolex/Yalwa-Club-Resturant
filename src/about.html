Fine Dining with Deep Roots

Yalwa Club was born from a simple belief: that Nigerian cuisine deserves the same reverence as the world's greatest culinary traditions. In 2019, Chef Aminu Garba — trained in Lagos, London, and Lyon — returned to Abuja with a vision to honour the flavours of his childhood with the rigour of fine dining.

We source over 85% of our ingredients locally: yams from Kogi farmers, fresh seafood from the Niger Delta, heirloom pepper varieties from Borno, and palm oil cold-pressed on our partnered estate in Cross River.

Beyond the food, Yalwa is a gathering place — for business dinners, anniversary celebrations, and Wednesday night jazz sessions that stretch past midnight. We believe the table is where community is built.

How We Got Here

Our Journey

2019

Doors Open

Yalwa Club opens its doors in the heart of Abuja with 12 tables and a menu of 24 dishes, all sourced within a 500km radius.

2020

Community Kitchen

Amid global uncertainty, we launched our Community Kitchen — feeding over 2,000 families across Abuja's underserved neighbourhoods.

2022

West Africa's Best New Restaurant

Recognised by Fine Dining Africa as one of the continent's most exciting culinary destinations, cementing Yalwa's place on the global map.

2024

The Jazz Sessions

We introduced our Wednesday night live jazz programme, pairing seasonal tasting menus with Nigeria's finest emerging musicians.
